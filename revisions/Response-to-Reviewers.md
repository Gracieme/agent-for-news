## 一、Assessing Chinese Through English（AP Chinese）— Minor Revision

### 意见 1：理论延伸 — epistemic injustice 从个人到制度/语言的哲学依据

**审稿人**：将 epistemic injustice 从“个人”延伸到“语言/制度”时，需说明集体/结构版本在哲学文献中的依据。

**作者回复**：  
We agree. We have added a focused subsection in the theoretical framework that grounds our use of hermeneutical injustice at the institutional level. We draw explicitly on Anderson (2012) on collective epistemic agency and Dotson (2012) on testimonial quieting as structural, and we limit our claim to *assessment discourse and the distribution of interpretive authority* over the tested language, not to hermeneutical injustice in all social domains.

**正文修改（建议插入 Theory 部分）**：

> Our use of *hermeneutical injustice* follows Fricker (2007) but extends to institutional epistemology (Anderson, 2012): the gap in collective interpretive resources can be sustained by institutional practices—here, the design and framing of the AP Chinese exam. We do not claim that Chinese speakers suffer hermeneutical injustice in all domains, but that *in this assessment regime* the authority to name, explain, and evaluate Chinese is allocated almost entirely to English and to test specifications, rendering Chinese “the object of explanation rather than a language of explanation” (see Results). This is consistent with Dotson’s (2012) notion of testimonial quieting at a structural level: the system pre-empts who can be heard as a knower about the language.

**需补文献**：Anderson (2012); Dotson (2012)；若已有则仅在正文中加如上限定句。

### 意见 3：反论点 — “structural logic can land unevenly” 需具体化

**审稿人**：建议用一两句说明 Spanish 与汉语在美国教育/修辞传统中的不同位置，使“落地不均”可验证。

**作者回复**：  
We have clarified the point in the Discussion. We now state that the same structural logic (L2 subordinated to English in test specifications) may land differently across languages because of differing institutional and rhetorical histories—e.g. the longer and more widespread presence of Spanish in U.S. schooling and high-stakes testing versus the more recent and narrower institutionalisation of Chinese. We present this as a hypothesis for future comparative work.

**正文修改（Discussion）**：

> The same structural logic—L2 subordinated to English as the metalanguage of control—may nevertheless *land unevenly* across languages. Spanish, for instance, has a longer and more diffuse history within U.S. education and high-stakes assessment; Chinese has been institutionalised more recently and often in narrower programmatic niches. We do not claim that AP Spanish is free of hermeneutical asymmetry, but that the *degree and kind* of marginalisation may differ in ways that warrant comparative study.

## 三、MICM（Translanguaging as Mediational Architecture）— Major Revision

### 意见 1：因果声称 — 将“translanguaging 是 primary mediational architecture”等改为假设表述

**审稿人**：在 11 人/21 片段规模下，应作为假设或理论驱动的解释，而非定论；结论需明确“待更大规模复制”。

**作者回复**：  
We fully agree. We have reframed our claims throughout as hypotheses and theoretically motivated interpretations. The Conclusion now explicitly states that the findings are offered as hypotheses for larger-scale replication, and we have replaced definitive formulations (e.g. “is the primary mediational architecture”) with formulations such as “may function as,” “the data suggest that,” and “is consistent with a mediational account.”

**正文修改（全文统一）**：

- 将 “Translanguaging is the primary mediational architecture” → “Translanguaging may function as the primary mediational architecture in these data” 或 “The data are consistent with translanguaging acting as a central mediational architecture.”
- 将 “The mechanism is not automatic. It depends on…” → “The data suggest that the mechanism may depend on…”
- 在 **Conclusion** 段首或段尾加：

> These findings are offered as *hypotheses* for larger-scale replication. Our design (N = 11, 21 episodes) does not support causal claims; we propose that the NoM–LRE–translanguaging linkage is a plausible, theoretically motivated account that should be tested in broader and more diverse samples.

---

### 意见 2：混淆因素 — instructor 差异必须讨论

**审稿人**：三门课三位不同 instructor 可能解释 C246 的 LRE 率等差异；需在 Limitations / Alternative explanations 中讨论。

**作者回复**：  
We have added a dedicated paragraph in Limitations (and, where relevant, in Alternative explanations) stating that the three courses were taught by different instructors and that instructor beliefs, practices, and norms regarding L1 use and translanguaging could plausibly affect the frequency and type of LREs and the observed NoM–LRE link. We recommend that future work either hold instructor constant or include instructor as a variable.

**正文修改（Limitations / Alternative explanations）**：

> An important limitation is that the three courses were taught by three different instructors. We did not control for instructor beliefs or practices regarding L1 use and translanguaging. It is plausible that instructor differences (e.g. in tolerating or encouraging L1, or in how they respond to NoM) could contribute to the variation we observed—including the lower LRE rate in C246. We therefore treat our findings as conditional on this sample and recommend that future studies either hold instructor constant or include instructor as an explicit factor in the design.

### 意见 3：方法 — “immediate NoM–LRE” 的操作定义

**审稿人**：请明确“immediate”是同一 turn、下一 turn，是否允许中间插入等，并与 Table 4/5 计数一致。

**作者回复**：  
We have added an operational definition of “immediate” in the Method section: an LRE was counted as immediately following a NoM if it occurred within the same speaker turn as the NoM or in the next turn, with no intervening change of topic. This definition was applied consistently to all counts reported in Tables 4 and 5.

**正文修改（Method）**：

> We defined an *immediate* NoM–LRE sequence as follows: the LRE began either in the same speaker turn as the NoM or in the immediately following turn, with no intervening topic shift or extended side sequence. All counts in Tables 4 and 5 use this definition. Sequences separated by more than one turn or by a clear topic change were coded as non-immediate and are reported separately [or “excluded from the immediate counts”].

## 四、You Need to Find the Guts（Charter DLI）— Major Revision

### 意见 1：单案例的贡献边界与推广性

**审稿人**：一个学校、一位教师，如何支撑“structural vulnerability”的普遍性；需明确本研究贡献是“结构逻辑的可读性”与“decoupled policy 的机制”，而非统计推广。

**作者回复**：  
We have clarified the scope of our claims in the Discussion (and, where appropriate, in the Introduction). We state explicitly that the contribution of this study is to make the *structural logic* of decoupled policy and teacher vulnerability *legible*—i.e. to name and analyse mechanisms that may operate in similar settings—rather than to generalise to all charter DLI schools. We cite Flyvbjerg and Stake on case-based generalisation and use Reddit data as recognition/illustration, not as a representative sample.

**正文修改（Discussion 开头或 Contribution 小节）**：

> This study does not aim to generalise to all charter or DLI contexts in a statistical sense. Its contribution is to make the *structural logic* of teacher vulnerability and decoupled policy *legible*: to show how “policy on the books” and “policy in practice” can diverge in ways that place teachers in an impossible bind, and to name the epistemic and emotional costs. We draw on Reddit and public discourse not as a representative sample but as further illustration that the *recognition* of these dynamics resonates beyond a single site. Whether and how these mechanisms operate in other schools remains a question for future research.
